Data deletion instructions

Last reviewed: July 18, 2026

Social Insights Studio provides provider disconnect and deletion paths for data that the service stores to operate analytics connections and reports.

Disconnect a provider

  1. Open the connected data source or workspace where the provider is authorized.
  2. Use the disconnect or revoke action for the provider if it is available in the product surface.
  3. For Facebook Pages or Instagram, open Connections and disconnect the selected resource. A shared Meta grant remains active while another selected Facebook or Instagram resource still uses it. Removing the final selected Meta resource attempts revocation and then deletes local Meta credentials, discovered resources, sync jobs, content, and insight snapshots even if provider revocation fails.
  4. For YouTube, open the workspace Connections view and choose Disconnect. The service attempts Google revocation first and then immediately purges all locally stored credentials, discovered channels, and YouTube snapshots tied to that authorization.
  5. For the current TikTok Looker Studio connector, revoke the connector in Looker Studio and, if needed, revoke the app from your TikTok account settings.

Request deletion

Email b.o.d@nixorcollege.edu.pk with the subject Social Insights Studio data deletion request. Include the provider, workspace or data source name, and the email address you used for the connection. Do not include access tokens or passwords. After identity and workspace authority are verified, authorized provider data is deleted promptly.

What deletion covers

  • Stored provider authorization records and encrypted provider tokens associated with your connection.
  • Provider resource records and stored analytics snapshots associated with the relevant workspace, where supported by the current product version.
  • Generated report artifacts that have not already expired under retention cleanup.

Provider-side revocation

You may also revoke Social Insights Studio in the relevant Facebook settings or in Google Account third-party connections. A valid Meta deauthorization or data-deletion callback purges the matching Facebook and Instagram authorization and returns an opaque confirmation status. Google revocation stops future access; when a bounded sync detects terminal Google revocation such as invalid_grant, the service immediately purges the local authorization and associated YouTube data. You may use the verified request path above instead of waiting for provider detection.
